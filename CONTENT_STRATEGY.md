## 1. Content Philosophy

Every word on the elReda Advertising website has one job: **move the visitor one step closer to contacting us.**

Content should educate, build trust, demonstrate expertise, and make it easy for the right client to say "yes."

### Core Principle
> Write for a business owner who is smart, busy, and skeptical. They've been burned by unreliable agencies before. They need to feel that elReda understands their problem, knows how to solve it, and can be trusted to deliver.

## 2. Brand Voice

### Tone of Voice

| Quality | What it means | What to avoid |
|---|---|---|
| **Confident** | Speak with authority — no hedging or over-qualifying | "We try to..." / "We hope to..." |
| **Clear** | Plain language — no jargon unless necessary | Technical terms without explanation |
| **Professional** | Polished, clean sentences | Casual slang or humor |
| **Direct** | Get to the point fast | Long-winded introductions |
| **Human** | Warm, not corporate — we're a partner, not a vendor | Cold, impersonal, generic copy |
| **Results-focused** | Always reference outcomes, not just features | "We design logos" vs "We build brands that attract clients" |

### Voice Examples

**Too weak:**
> "We offer a range of design services that can help your business look better."

**Too corporate:**
> "We leverage human-centered design methodologies to optimize brand perception across all touchpoints."

**elReda voice:**
> "Your brand is the first thing clients judge. We make sure they judge it well."

## 5. Service Page Copy Framework

Each service page follows this content structure:

### Section 1: Service Hero

**Formula:**
```
H1: [Service Name] That [Business Outcome]
Sub: 1–2 sentences explaining who it's for and what we deliver
CTA: "Get a Quote" | "Chat on WhatsApp"
```

**Example — Branding:**
```
H1: Brand Identity Design That Builds Trust and Drives Growth
Sub: We create complete brand identities that make your business look professional, stand out from competitors, and attract the right clients.
```

### Section 2: What We Offer

- Bullet-point deliverables
- No jargon — describe each deliverable in terms of benefit
- Include the technical name + what it means for the client

### Section 3: Our Process

Step format:
```
Step 1 — Discovery
We start by understanding your business, your goals, your competitors, and your audience. No assumptions.

Step 2 — Strategy
...
```

### Section 4: Why elReda for [Service]

3 specific reasons, each with:
- Short headline
- 1–2 sentence explanation
- Focus on outcome, not feature

### Section 5: FAQ

5–8 questions following natural search query phrasing:
- "How much does [service] cost in Egypt?"
- "How long does [service] take?"
- "What do I need to prepare before starting?"
- "Do you offer revisions?"
- "Can you work with businesses outside Cairo?"

## 6. Portfolio Case Study Framework

Each case study must tell a story — not just show images.

### Structure

```
PROJECT OVERVIEW
Client: [Name or "Confidential — Restaurant Industry, Cairo"]
Services: [List]
Timeline: [X weeks/months]
Year: [Year]

THE CHALLENGE
[2–3 paragraphs explaining what problem the client had and why it mattered]

OUR APPROACH
[How we thought about the problem, what research we did, what strategy we chose]

THE PROCESS
[How we worked: sketches, concepts, revisions, final direction]

THE RESULT
[Final deliverables described in terms of what they achieve]
[Images, gallery, before/after]

THE IMPACT
[If available: metrics, client feedback, before/after business results]

CLIENT TESTIMONIAL
"[Quote]" — Client Name, Position, Company
```

### Writing Rules for Case Studies

- Always explain the "why" behind creative decisions
- Describe outcomes in business terms: "increased brand recognition," "improved conversion," "reduced operational cost"
- Never say "beautiful" or "stunning" without explaining what the design achieves
- Keep business goals central to every paragraph

## 7. Blog Content Plan

### Content Pillars

#### Pillar 1 — Branding & Identity
Target audience: Business owners who know they need branding but don't know where to start

Articles:
- "How to build a strong brand identity for your business in Egypt"
- "Logo design vs. brand identity: what's the difference?"
- "5 signs your brand needs a redesign"
- "How to choose the right colors for your brand"
- "Why inconsistent branding is costing your business money"

#### Pillar 2 — Website Development
Target audience: Business owners searching for a website but unsure of costs, options, or process

Articles:
- "How much does a business website cost in Egypt? (2026 guide)"
- "Shopify vs WooCommerce: which is better for your online store?"
- "10 things your business website must have to generate leads"
- "Landing page vs. full website: what does your business need?"
- "How to choose a web development agency in Egypt"

#### Pillar 3 — Technology & AI
Target audience: Business owners interested in automation and systems

Articles:
- "What is ERP and does your business need one?"
- "How AI can automate repetitive tasks in your business"
- "WhatsApp Business API: what is it and how can it grow your business?"
- "5 business processes you can automate today"
- "How to use AI chatbots for customer service without losing the human touch"

#### Pillar 4 — Digital Marketing
Target audience: Business owners running or considering paid ads and social media

Articles:
- "How to run Meta Ads that actually generate leads"
- "SEO for Egyptian businesses: a beginner's guide"
- "How to create a social media strategy for a small business"
- "WhatsApp marketing: how to reach more clients without being spammy"

#### Pillar 5 — Business Growth
Target audience: Founders and SME owners thinking strategically

Articles:
- "How to go from offline to online: a step-by-step guide"
- "5 digital investments that pay off fastest for small businesses"
- "What is digital transformation and what does it mean for your business?"
- "How to create a professional company profile that wins clients"

### Blog Post Format

Every article must include:

```
[Meta Title] — 50–60 characters, includes primary keyword
[Meta Description] — 150–160 characters, includes keyword + CTA

[H1] — Matches or varies the meta title
[Introduction] — Hook + what the reader will learn + why it matters

[H2 sections] — 3–6 sections covering the topic comprehensively
[Images] — At least 2–3 relevant images with alt text
[Internal links] — Minimum 2 links to relevant service pages
[FAQ section] — 3–5 questions (FAQ schema)

[Conclusion] — Summary + next step CTA
[CTA box] — "Need help with [topic]? Let's talk." → WhatsApp or Quote form
```

## 9. CMS Content Guidelines

### Tone for Each Content Type

| Content Type | Tone | Length |
|---|---|---|
| Homepage hero | Bold, exciting, concise | 1 headline + 1–2 sentence sub |
| Service page | Educational, confident, results-focused | 600–1,200 words |
| Case study | Storytelling, specific, honest | 800–1,500 words |
| Blog post | Educational, helpful, accessible | 1,000–2,000 words |
| FAQ answer | Direct, clear, complete | 50–150 words per answer |
| Testimonial | Authentic, specific, outcome-focused | 2–4 sentences |
| Button/CTA | Action-oriented, no passive voice | 2–5 words |

### CTA Copy Standards

**Strong CTAs:**
- "Request a Quote" ✓
- "Chat on WhatsApp" ✓
- "See Our Work" ✓
- "Book a Free Consultation" ✓
- "Start Your Project" ✓

**Weak CTAs to avoid:**
- "Click Here" ✗
- "Learn More" (when used generically) ✗
- "Submit" (on forms) → replace with "Send My Request" ✓

## 10. Arabic Content Standards

- All Arabic content must be professionally written — not machine-translated
- Use Modern Standard Arabic (MSA) for professionalism — not dialect
- Arabic content should be fully localized, not just translated (examples, references, pricing context should reflect the Egyptian/Arab market)
- RTL text must be proofread by a native Arabic speaker
- Arabic SEO keywords should be integrated naturally — not stuffed
- Arabic blog content targets Egyptian and GCC business owners equally
- Tajawal font provides excellent readability for all Arabic content

---

## 11. Accessibility in Content

- Use plain language — max reading level: grade 8 equivalent
- Short sentences: max 25 words
- Short paragraphs: max 4 sentences
- Headings must be descriptive — no "Introduction" or "Section 1"
- All images must have descriptive alt text
- All links must have descriptive anchor text — no "click here"
- Video content must have captions or transcripts

---

*This strategy ensures every piece of content serves a business purpose and contributes to the overall goal of turning visitors into qualified leads.*
